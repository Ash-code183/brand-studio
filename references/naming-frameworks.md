# Brand Naming Frameworks

## The 5 Strategies in Detail

### 1. Compound Words
Fuse two semantically relevant words. One word grounds it, one word elevates it.

Rules:
- Both words should be pronounceable together without friction
- Avoid compound words longer than 3 syllables total
- The compound should feel inevitable in retrospect, not forced
- Check: can you say it 3x fast? Is it easy to spell after hearing it?

Good pattern: [VERB + NOUN], [NOUN + NOUN], [ADJ + NOUN]
Examples: Dropbox (drop + box), Snapchat (snap + chat), Salesforce (sales + force)

Bad patterns to avoid: three-word compounds, hyphenated compounds

### 2. Invented / Coined Words
Create a phonesthetically pleasing word from scratch, or morph an existing word.

Sub-strategies:
- **Morphed**: Take a real word and alter 1-2 letters (Tumblr, Lyft, Fiverr)
- **Portmanteau**: Blend two words at their natural phoneme boundaries (Pinterest = pin + interest)
- **Phonetic invention**: Build from consonant clusters that feel energetic (Zynga, Kaggle, Quora)

Rules:
- Must be pronounceable without instruction
- Must be unique — no similar existing brands
- Ideally: easy to Google (unique letter combination)
- Avoid: double letters that obscure pronunciation (Xyzzy), awkward consonant clusters

### 3. Metaphor Names
A word borrowed from another domain that evokes the transformation or feeling.

Sub-strategies:
- **Nature metaphors**: Amazon, Nest, Sprout (growth, ecosystem, living systems)
- **Journey metaphors**: Slack (relaxed velocity), Arc (trajectory), Linear (direction)
- **Physical object metaphors**: Stripe (clean, defined), Box (container), Frame (structure)

Rules:
- The metaphor should resonate when explained, not require explanation upfront
- Avoid overused metaphors: Apex, Summit, Peak, Bridge, Nexus, Pivot, Catalyst
- The word should be able to "become" the brand (prime candidates: short, visual, ownable)

### 4. Founder-Coded / Authority Names
Signals expertise, heritage, or category ownership.

Sub-strategies:
- **Founder name**: McKinsey, Bloomberg, Deloitte (founder's credibility as the brand)
- **Category ownership**: Shopify (shop + modify = own your shop), HubSpot (hub of activity)
- **Origin-coded**: Yokel (local focus), Harvest (agricultural tool → time tracking)
- **Acronym**: IBM, IDEO, SAP (only works if the acronym is pronounceable or memorable)

Rules:
- Avoid generic authority words: Solutions, Systems, Global, Enterprise, Pro, Plus
- Founder name only works if the founder is the credibility signal
- Category ownership requires the word to be defensible

### 5. Short Punchy Nouns
1-2 syllable words with inherent energy. The name IS the brand.

Rules:
- Must be a real word or very close to one
- Single syllable preferred: Stripe, Hims, Figma, Bose, Lyra
- Two syllables max: Notion, Linear, Asana, Vercel
- Should feel like it belongs in the same sentence as the product
- Avoid: words that already have strong negative or irrelevant associations

---

## Scoring Criteria

Rate each candidate 1-5 on:
- **Recall**: Can you remember it 24 hours later?
- **Spell**: Can you spell it after hearing it?
- **Say**: Is it pleasant to say aloud, in a pitch, on a phone call?
- **Own**: Is the .com available (or close enough)?
- **Fit**: Does it feel right for the category and brand personality?

Total 25 = perfect. Shortlist anything scoring 18+.

---

## Red Flags

Reject any name that:
- Contains "tech", "soft", "labs", "ai", "io" as a word (not a TLD)
- Is longer than 3 syllables
- Sounds like an existing brand (legal risk)
- Relies on a pun that only works in English
- Is a common dictionary word with no ownable angle (Table, Store, Work)
- Ends in "-ify", "-ly", "-fy", "-hub" (massively overused)
